Non-Fictional Books

"The Let Them Theory" by Mel Robbins
Mel Robbins' "The Let Them Theory" delves into personal development strategies, encouraging readers to embrace authenticity and overcome self-imposed limitations. Its practical advice and motivational insights have resonated with a wide audience, securing its position at the top of bestseller lists.

"Seven Things You Can't Say About China" by Tom Cotton
In "Seven Things You Can't Say About China," Tom Cotton offers a critical examination of China's global influence and the challenges it poses to Western democracies. The book's provocative analysis has sparked discussions, contributing to its strong sales.

"The Technological Republic" by Alexander C. Karp and Nicholas W. Zamiska
Authored by Alexander C. Karp and Nicholas W. Zamiska, "The Technological Republic" explores the intersection of technology, governance, and society. The authors provide insights into how technological advancements this books is best for general knowledge.

"Dinner at Our Place" by Shiza Shahid
Shiza Shahid's "Dinner at Our Place" is a culinary memoir that intertwines recipes with stories, celebrating the power of food to bring people together. The book's heartfelt narrative and delectable recipes have made it a favorite among readers, leading to its bestseller status.

"Lorne" by Susan Morrison
In "Lorne," Susan Morrison presents a biography of Lorne Michaels, the creator and producer of "Saturday Night Live." The book offers an in-depth look at Michaels' life and his impact on television comedy, appealing to fans of the show and those interested in the entertainment industry.
